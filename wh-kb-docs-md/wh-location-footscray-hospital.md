## Key information

The Footscray Hospital Emergency Department (ED) is located at 20 Geelong Road and is open 24 hours a day. You can access the ED from both 89 Ballarat Road and 20 Geelong Road, Footscray. A vehicle drop off zone is clearly signed in front of the ED entrance.

Patients are assessed on arrival, and treatment is prioritised based on medical urgency. Those with severe injuries or illnesses will receive care first.

We will continue to be an adult Emergency Department and like now, unless critically unwell, most children will be redirected to one of the nearby paediatric emergency departments.

## At triage

On arrival, you will be assessed by a nurse to decide how serious your problem could be.  This system called ‘triage’ is used Australia-wide to make sure patients with possible life-threatening problems are seen first. The same process occurs if you come to the ED by ambulance.

After triage, clerical staff will check details like your address, phone number, next of kin and local doctor, to make sure we have the correct information on our system.

Once complete, depending on your condition, you may wait in the waiting room or be moved to an area inside the department for further care. There are rooms and staff available to provide simple treatments and start tests whilst you are waiting.

## Inside the acute zone

Most people coming to ED will be cared for in the acute assessment area.

Doctors, nurses and other staff will work together to make you feel better and do tests to find the diagnosis or rule-out serious causes.

Senior nurses and specialist doctors supervise your care as well as keep an eye on the people arriving and waiting.

Most people who come to ED will go home that day. Only a few people need further care in hospital, and some may be able to receive care at home through hospital services.

Our aim is to provide timely quality care that considers your health and social needs.

## Transport and parking

There are 10-minute drop-off bays in front of the Emergency Department. These can be accessed from both 20 Geelong Road as well as 89 Ballarat Road. Parking is available in the hospital car park, accessible from both Geelong and Ballarat Roads. Drop-off and parking is free within the first 30 minutes.

## Main areas of treatment

The Emergency Department comprises 24 acute, 3 resus, 9 fast-track and 16 short stay cubicles. The 8-bed Mental Health and Alcohol and Other Drugs Hub is due to open later in 2026.

## Alternatives to the emergency department

For non-life-threatening conditions like infections, fevers, nausea, minor cuts, rashes, burns, or fractures, consider the following free options

### Victorian Virtual Emergency Department (Virtual ED)

A 24/7, video-based healthcare service.

Connect with senior emergency nurses and doctors via your smartphone, computer, or tablet. They can provide medical advice, prescribe medications, and arrange tests close to home.

Visit the patient section of the [Victorian Virtual Emergency Department website](https://www.vved.org.au/welcome/) to get started. The Virtual ED is free for all Victorians.

### Urgent Care clinics

Face to face consultations and open early to late, 7 days a week.

No Medicare card, appointment or referral is required, but bookings can be made online if desired.

The closest clinics to Footscray ED are in Maribyrnong and Moonee Ponds.

- [Maribyrnong Medicare Urgent Care Clinic / Maribyrnong Priority Primary Care Centre (maribyrnongmucc.com.au)](https://www.maribyrnongmucc.com.au/)
- [Moonee Ponds Urgent Care Clinic - Urgent Care Network | ForHealth Group (urgentcarenetworkaustralia.com.au)](https://www.urgentcarenetworkaustralia.com.au/location/moonee-ponds-urgent-care-clinic)

## Inpatient rooms and wards

Footscray Hospital offers large, modern inpatient rooms designed to help patients feel comfortable throughout their stay.

Each ward includes a mix of single and twin rooms, featuring shared or private ensuites, Smart TVs, and views of the surrounding parks and suburbs. Rooms are also equipped with a small pull-out bed.

Each ward has a family lounge and an enclosed balcony accessible to both patients and visitors. These spaces offer quiet areas to support recovery.

## Parking

The public car park can be accessed at both 89 Ballarat Road, Footscray, and 20 Geelong Road, Footscray.

The public car park has dedicated motorbike parking bays on the Ground level.

Bicycle racks for public use are located near the main entrance of the hospital and near the Emergency Department.

The public car park uses licence plate recognition to grant users access to the car park.

Car park users can pay at automated pay machines, and this will be recognised by the system on exit. If the user has not paid, they will be asked to pay at the exit using their bank payment card.

Pensioners can validate their parking by showing their concession at the main reception, where they will receive a validation ticket. They can then pay at the machine with their validated ticket and with cash or card.

## Check-in kiosks

Patients at Footscray Hospital can check-in for their appointments using the check-in kiosks, located in the main reception on the ground level or at entrances to clinics and other units. The services that use the check-in system are:

- clinics 2,3, and 4 (Adult Specialist Clinics)
- medical imaging
- allied health
- clinic 11
- planned surgery admissions

To use the check-in kiosk, please either:

- scan the barcode on your appointment letter
- swipe your Medicare card
- type in your details on the kiosk.

You will then be asked to confirm your details, and you’ll receive an appointment confirmation, a printed ticket, and a text message to your phone.

When it is time to go to your appointment, you will receive a text message to your phone, and a number on the queuing screen will appear in the waiting area of your appointment location.

## Lifts

Footscray Hospital has two sets of lifts, named in consultation with the Wurundjeri Woi-wurrung people – the traditional owners of the land on which the hospital precinct stands.

### Mareingalk lifts

Mareingalk is the name for Maribyrnong and the nearby Maribyrnong River.

The blue-tiled Mareingalk Lifts are to the left of the main reception. The Mareingalk Lifts rise to Level 4.

### Bundha lifts

Bundha means unity – because the hospital brings people together from all walks of life.

The green-tiled Bundha Lifts are to the right of the main reception. The Bundha Lifts rise to Level 10.

Footscray Hospital is designed to provide amenities and services to patients, consumers and the wider Footscray community.

Within the hospital precinct, the village green is a central open space with a small playgound that unites the buildings.

The hospital has eight cafés, a general store with Australia Post services, a retail pharmacy, a gift shop, and an opportunity shop. The hospital precinct also includes a childcare centre, private medical clinics, a gym, the Wilim Berrbang Aboriginal Health Unit, and a community centre for meetings and events.
